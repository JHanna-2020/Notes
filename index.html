Types of Government

Government Distribution of Power:

  • Unitary - Power is held by the central (national) government.

  • Confederation - A group of countries or states that come together for certain things. The confederate government (central) makes decisions only on issues that affect the entire confederation.

  • Federal - Divides power between the central (natioonal) government and the local (regional) governments. Most federal systems give a lot of power to the lower governments to handle local affairs. The centeral (national) government handles issues that concern the entire country, such as the military or negotiating treaties with foreign countries.

Citizen Participation in Government:

  • Autocracy - Government in which one person has unlimited power and citizens have little to no power.

  • Oligarchy - Government by a small group of people or a political party. Citizens have a very limited role

  • Democracy - Government in which the supreme power is vested in the people and exercised by them directly or indirectly.

  • Direct Democracy - People govern themselves by voting on issues individually as citizens (mob role)

  • Representative Democracy - People elect representatives and give them responsibility and power to make the laws.

Forms of Government

  • Parliamentary Democracy - A parliamentary system is led by representatives of the people. Members of the executive branch are also members of the legislative branch.

  • Presidential Democracy - A system of government in which the president is constitutionally independent of the legislature.

  • Totalitarian - A country with only one political party. People are forced to do what the government tells them to and may also be prevented from leaving the country.

Types of Governments and Examples

  • Anarchy - is a situation where there is no government

  • Dictatorship – (North Korea) is a country ruled by a single leader. May use force to keep control.

  • Absolute Monarchy – (Saudi Arabia) has a king, queen, emperor, or empress. The monarch has absolute power.

  • Constitutional Monarchy – (England) The monarch is the head of state, however he or she is legally bound by the national constitution.

  • Theocracy – Rulers claim to be ruling on behalf of a set of religious ideas, or as direct agents of deity.

  • Republic – power of the central government is restricted, the states or provinces have some self-government, and ultimate power is held by the voters who choose their representatives. (Rule of Law).

Influences on Our Constitution

Ancient Judaism:

  • The laws of Moses (Torah of Moses) refers to the “five books” that were used to set the precedent for law including: moral laws, social laws, and food laws.

  • The influence of Biblical laws can be found in the legal and ethical codes throughout different aspects of national, state, & local governments.

  • Moses stressed that people should seek to create a just society based on respect for the law.

  • Colonial thinkers based their notion of justice on this idea

· • • • • Colonists, who were influenced by their faith, believed in natural law, the idea that a universal set of moral principles existed. • Many colonists believed that a human law that violated natural law was unjust and should be changed. Ancient: u Greeks – Ancient Greeks introduced the idea of direct democracy, or decision making by all citizens. u Direct democracy took root in New England’s town meetings, where citizens gathered to solve local problems. Romans – From ancient Romans came the idea of representative government, or decision-making by elected officials.
